Startup India — Seed Fund Scheme (SISFS)
Early-stage financial support for proof-of-concept, prototype development and market trials.
Grant / Seed
Implementing: DPIIT / Startup India
What & Who
SISFS provides seed support through empanelled incubators/seed funds to startups to help them reach prototype & market stage. Eligibility follows Startup India / DPIIT norms (early-stage, innovative startups).
Eligibility checklist
  • Recognised as a startup under Startup India (DPIIT) or meets Startup India definition.
  • At idea/prototype stage needing funds for POC, prototype or trials.
  • Team documents and project summary required.
Common documents: 1-page project summary, founders' IDs, pitch deck, prototype evidence (if any).
How to apply
Apply via empanelled incubators/seed funds or visit the official SISFS portal.
Open SISFS Portal Checklist
Tip
Attach mentor/institution endorsement to increase chances; keep budget 3–6 months.
Atal Innovation Mission — Atal Incubation Centres (AIC)
AICs provide incubation, mentoring, infrastructure and networking support to startups.
Incubation • Mentorship
Managed by AIM (NITI Aayog)
What & Who
AIM supports AICs (set up by institutions/organisations) and startups can apply to AICs for incubation, mentoring and lab access.
Eligibility checklist (for startups)
  • Student/early-stage teams with an idea or prototype.
  • Submission of idea summary and team details to local AIC.
Docs: Idea summary, team profile, mentor/supporting institution details (if any).
How to apply / find AIC
Search for nearest AIC and contact the centre for application details; organisations can apply to AIM to set up AIC per AIM guidelines.
AIC Guidelines (PDF) Request Help
Tip
If in school, ask principal to connect with local AIC or AIM programmes.
NIDHI — NIDHI Seed Support Program (NIDHI-SSP)
DST program providing seed support through selected incubators to early startups.
Seed Grant
Managed by: Department of Science & Technology (DST)
What & Who
NIDHI-SSP funds startups via incubators—incubators identify deserving startups and provide seed funds and hand-holding.
Eligibility checklist
  • Startup selected by a NIDHI supported incubator.
  • Must fulfil incubator-specific criteria (incubator will guide documentation).
Docs: Incubator application, project summary, team profiles, incubator recommendations.
How to apply
Approach a NIDHI-supported incubator (list available on NIDHI portal) and apply for seed support through them.
NIDHI-SSP (official) Incubators list
Tip
Join a recognised incubator — they handle most of the application process.
CGTMSE — Credit Guarantee for MSMEs
Guarantee cover for collateral-free loans to micro & small enterprises — helps get bank loans without security.
Credit Guarantee
Operated by CGTMSE (MSME & SIDBI)
What & Who
CGTMSE provides guarantee to lending institutions for loans to Micro & Small Enterprises (helps startups/SMEs access finance without collateral).
Eligibility checklist
  • Borrowers to obtain PAN / Udyam registration as required by lender.
  • Loan must be from an eligible lending institution registered with CGTMSE.
Docs: Loan application, PAN, project/term loan docs as required by bank.
How to apply
Approach a bank/DFI — if bank is registered with CGTMSE they will process guarantee as part of loan sanction.
CGTMSE Official Bank contact tip
Tip
Get Udyam / GST set up before applying for bank loans — speeds up process.
Pradhan Mantri Mudra Yojana (PMMY)
Microloans (Shishu/Kishore/Tarun) for non-corporate small businesses, through banks/nbfc/cooperative.
Microloan
Support: Mudra / Banks
What & Who
PMMY gives collateral-free loans up to specified limits under Shishu/Kishore/Tarun categories to micro / small entrepreneurs.
Eligibility checklist
  • Small business or micro-enterprise (non-corporate) requiring capital for operations/working capital.
  • No prior credit history not mandatory — bank will assess viability.
Docs: Business plan/summary, ID, address proof, Udyam/GST (if available).
How to apply
Approach a bank or log in to MyScheme / Udyam portals; many banks accept online apps for Mudra loans.
PMMY Info (Mudra) Bank tip
Tip
Start small (Shishu) if new — build track record, then scale to larger loans.
SIDBI — Fund of Funds (Support to VC funds for startups)
Government-authorised Fund-of-Funds (managed via SIDBI) that invests in daughter VC funds which then back startups.
Indirect Funding
Managed by: SIDBI / GoI
What & Who
FFS provides capital to VC funds (daughter funds). Startups can get funded if they are selected by these daughter funds; this is an indirect route to VC capital backed by government corpus.
Eligibility checklist (for startups)
  • Startup must meet the startup definition and be investment-ready.
  • Apply / pitch to VC funds (daughter funds) supported under the FFS program.
Docs: Pitch deck, financials, traction metrics as required by VC.
How to access
Track daughter funds listed by SIDBI and approach them; startup applications handled by VC funds.
SIDBI — FFS Info Find funds
Tip
Get investor-ready: traction, metrics and clear unit economics help getting VC attention.
